I. The Core Discovery

All cycles of time are geometrically identical

The central insight of the Universe Time Wheel is that every recurring time cycle — a second, a minute, an hour, a day, a year — is geometrically the same object: one complete rotation of 360°. The only variable is scale.

By mapping multiple independent time scales to a single circular face, any one angular measurement on the face simultaneously encodes the full state of time across all scales. The wheel is not a display — it is a geometric calculator.

Key ratio: 365 days ÷ 360° = 1.0139 days per degree of arc

Orbital angular rate: Earth travels 0.9863° per solar day

Quarter markers (exact): 91.25 · 182.50 · 273.75 · 365 days — not rounded to 90°

Cardinal anchors: North = Winter solstice · East = Spring equinox · South = Summer solstice · West = Autumn equinox

This is not an approximation. The numbers inscribed on the outer ring are exact fractional day-counts of Earth's orbital quarters. No prior clock or calendar instrument unifies these four scales — orbital year, solar day, civil hour, and cultural time — on a single readable face.

II. Architecture

Four rings, four civilisations of time

The instrument consists of four concentric annular rings, each representing a complete and independent timekeeping tradition. All four share the same rotational geometry, enabling simultaneous cross-system reading.

Ring Cycle One Full Rotation Key Markers
Orbital Earth's solar orbit 365 days (1 year) N/S/E/W = solstices & equinoxes
24-hour Solar day 24 hours Hours 0–23
12-hour Civil half-day 12 hours Hours 1–12, AM/PM
Swahili Saa Sunrise-based day 12 saa (sunrise to sunset) Saa 1 (moja) = 7 AM Western

The innermost ring encodes Saa za Kiswahili — the timekeeping system of East African coastal cultures — in which the day begins at sunrise rather than midnight. The Swahili numbers 1–12 are rotationally offset by six positions from the Western 12-hour ring, so both systems are readable from the same hand position.

III. The Cultural Layer

Saa za Kiswahili — time from sunrise

The integration of Swahili time is not decorative. It represents a distinct philosophical framework: time counted from the natural anchor of sunrise, not from the arbitrary civil midnight. The Universe Time Wheel is the first instrument to display both frameworks simultaneously and geometrically.

The highlighted cell updates live. Both the Western 12-hour position and the Swahili saa position are read from a single hand — the geometry of the rings handles the conversion automatically, with no calculation required by the reader.

IV. Potential Patent Claims

What may be protectable

The following represent potential independent claims for a utility patent application. These should be reviewed and refined with a registered patent attorney. The core novelty lies in the geometric unification of independent time systems on a single instrument.

  • 1
    Unified concentric time display — A timekeeping instrument comprising four or more concentric annular rings, each encoding a distinct cyclical time system sharing a common rotational axis, wherein a single angular measurement of a pointer simultaneously yields a valid reading in all encoded time systems.
  • 2
    Orbital-to-calendar ring — An annular ring encoding Earth's solar orbital position in degrees, wherein cardinal compass directions N, S, E, W correspond to solstices and equinoxes respectively, and wherein quarter-year markers are inscribed at the exact fractional values 91.25, 182.50, 273.75, and 365 days.
  • 3
    Simultaneous multi-system readout — A method of reading time wherein a single indicator position, expressed as an angle θ from a fixed reference, yields the time of day in a 12-hour civil system, the time of day in a 24-hour solar system, the day of the year in an orbital system, and the cultural time in a sunrise-referenced system, all without arithmetic.
  • 4
    Swahili cultural integration — A clock face incorporating a Swahili saa ring wherein Swahili hour numerals 1–12 are angularly offset from Western hour numerals 1–12 by six hour-positions (180°), enabling simultaneous reading of Western civil time and Swahili sunrise-referenced time from the same pointer position.
  • 5
    Physical embodiment — A physical clock device comprising the concentric ring system described above, wherein each ring is fabricated from a distinct material to visually differentiate time systems (e.g. brass, anodized aluminium, acrylic, wood), and wherein the device includes at minimum three hands operating at independent angular velocities corresponding to hour, day-of-year, and sub-hour resolution.

Note on prior art: Standard clock faces encode one or two time systems (12h and 24h). Astronomical clocks (e.g. Prague Orloj) encode celestial position but do not unify civil and cultural time systems. No known instrument encodes Swahili saa time geometrically alongside orbital, 24-hour, and 12-hour civil time on a single readable face.

Design patent: The specific visual arrangement, proportions, and material language of the four-ring face may also be protectable as a separate design patent.
